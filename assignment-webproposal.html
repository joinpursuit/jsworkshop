Assignment: Web Application Proposal

As part of this workshop, you will create and present a proposal for a web app you would like to build. A web app is a computer program that runs within a web browser such as Chrome, and uses HTML, CSS, and Javascript to respond to users. In developing this proposal, you will try your hand at product design, which is a part of the Access Code program.

You will develop the proposal in two stages.

Concept Statement — individual homework

As a homework assignment due at the start of Day 2, you will choose a problem that you are passionate about, research ideas for how you might address this problem with a web app, and write up a Concept Statement.

Proposal — team project

During Day 2 of the workshop, you will work in a team to develop a complete proposal. Each team will consider the Concept Statements of its members and choose one, then work together to develop it into a Proposal.

Concept Statement (individual)

The Concept Statement is assigned during Day 1 of the workshop and due at the beginning of Day 2.

Description

Think of a problem that is personally important to you. It may be a problem you've experienced yourself, or that affects a community you're part of, or that relates to a hobby, interest, cause, or passion important to you. Then think about how a web app could address the problem. The problem does not need to be deeply profound (you don't have to cure cancer!), but choose one that you are passionate about.

Once you've chosen a problem, research existing solutions to the problem, particularly web sites, web apps, and mobile apps that might already address it. Think about what works well about these, and what doesn't work as well. Then think about how you would like to solve the problem using a web app.

Your Concept Statement should include at least these sections:

  • The name of your Concept Statement, and your name.

  • A Problem Statement, providing context and explaining in detail what the problem is.

  • A summary of Existing Solutions to the problem, which may be technological or otherwise.

  • One or more User Stories. A user story is a description of who a user of the app might be, why s/he is using the app, and how s/he interacts with it to meet his/her goals. The user could be someone you know, or an imaginary typical user.

  • A Concept for the web app, explaining its major features and how they work together to address the problem better than the existing solutions.

Feel free to add additional sections to your Concept Statements to represent additional insights and ideas you might have.

A sample Concept Statement is here. Your Concept Statement does not need to look exactly like the sample, but make sure you include the sections above.

Deliverables

  1. Email a PDF of your Concept Statement to accesscode@c4q.nyc by 10 AM on Day 2.

  2. Plan to present your Concent Statement to your team. You will have about 5 minutes to present and discuss it. Focus on your problem statement and user stories.

  3. Be ready to hear, evaluate, and improve your team members' Concept Statements.

Proposal (team)

You will work on the Proposal as part of a team during Day 2 of the workshop. Each team will submit one proposal.

Description

The Proposal should include the following sections.

  • The name of the Proposal and the names of team members.

  • The Problem Statement, Existing Solutions, and User Stories. The team may choose to improve these sections from the original Concept Statement.

  • Features & Interactivity, which describes features of the app, and all the ways a user can interact with it. Remember that a web app is not just a static web page with fixed text and pictures; a web app responds to user inputs and actions with sophisticated behaviors.

  • Mockups of the pages. A mockup is a rough sketch of what the page looks like.

Feel free to include any additional material you feel improves the proposal.

Example

A sample Proposal is here. Your Proposal does not need to look exactly like the sample, but make sure you include the sections above.

Process

During the teamwork part of Day 2, each team will follow this process to develop the Proposal.

  1. (30 min) Each member of the team presents his/her Concept Statement to other members of the team. Team members ask questions to clarify the m and the proposed solution.

  2. (10 min) The team as a whole chooses one Concept Statement to develop into a web app Proposal.

  3. (20 min) The team works together to refine the problem statement and user story, and to choose the features that will be part of the web app Proposal.

  4. (90 min) The team works to develop and write up the Proposal. The team may work together or in subteams. (Please do not work individually.) It is up to each team to organize the work requried to create the Proposal.

One member of each team should email a PDF of the Proposal to accesscode@c4q.nyc at the end of the team session.

Tools

Please use any tools you find useful. The web provides many free tools you can use. For example:

  • Google Docs is a great way to prepare the Concept Statement and Proposal documents, including text and pictures. There's a convenient "Download as PDF" feature which you can use to submit your Proposal.
  • Many drawing, sketching, and image editing apps can be helpful in making sketches.
  • Google Image Search can help you find images to incorporate in your designs.
  • There are several free tools available especially for creating mockups of web and mobile apps.
  • Some online web design tools (for example, balsamiq) have free trials.

Evaluation

Your Concept Statement and Proposal will be evaluated on:

  • How thoroughly did you think through your the problem, user stories, design, and features?
  • How thoroughly did you research existing solutions?
  • Did you prepare a carefully-written writeup?
  • How creative is your solution to the problem?
  • Did you search for, find, and use online resources and tools to improve your proposal?

Proposals will not be evaluated on aesthetics, nor on specifics of the problem you choose to address.

Further, your teamwork will be evaluated on:

  • Do you present your Concept Statement clearly to your team?
  • Do you work effectively as part of a team?
  • Do you teach and learn from your teammates?
  • Are you respectful of your teammates?
  • Do listen carefully to your teammates?
  • Do you provide constructive suggestions and accept your teammates' suggestions?
